Vision & research directions

Observation

Many contemporary AI systems rely on short-lived, stateless interactions. Despite strong short-term performance, they often struggle to maintain a coherent internal state, accumulate durable experience, or reason across extended time horizons.

This limitation constrains long-term, goal-directed behavior and prevents the construction of progressively structured internal representations of the world.

Vision

L-TOPIC explores cognitive architectures capable of maintaining persistent internal state, integrating experience over time, and reasoning across multiple, evolving objectives.

The project follows a progressive, experimental research methodology, aiming to understand how AGI-like functional properties may emerge from clearly identified architectural components, without premature claims.

Research directions

1. Persistent memory

Mechanisms to preserve knowledge, state, and experience beyond single interactions.

2. Internal world models

Structured internal representations to reason beyond immediate input.

3. Long-horizon reasoning

Planning and reasoning across extended time horizons with multiple objectives.

4. Cognitive continuity

Maintaining internal coherence as context, objectives, and knowledge evolve.

Positioning

L-TOPIC does not position itself as a completed Artificial General Intelligence. It adopts a cautious, functional approach focused on exploring, measuring, and understanding AGI-related properties without marketing-driven claims.

The project emphasizes conceptual clarity, reproducibility, and incremental evaluation of hypotheses.
