Vision & functional criteria

L-TOPIC is built for long-running work: projects that require continuity, decision-making, and control over time. Our approach is product-oriented and deep-tech: we define capabilities, measure them, and iterate.

Reframing “AGI”: human-level, not super-intelligence

We consider the “AGI = super-intelligence that understands everything” narrative to be a conceptual confusion. Human intelligence is not omniscient, universal, or all-powerful. A human does not know everything, cannot do everything, and is not “general” in an absolute sense.

A competent human is situated, contextual, and specialized through experience. That is the reference we find most relevant.

L-TOPIC does not aim for an abstract super-intelligence.
It aims for intelligence comparable to a real human: situated, specialized, and able to decide effectively in concrete situations.

Positioning

The term AGI has no single scientific definition today. Many actors define their own criteria (memory, autonomy, planning, continuity), and then claim “AGI” according to those criteria.

Under common functional criteria (persistent memory, long-horizon multi-goal reasoning, continuity of execution), L-TOPIC can be considered AGI-like. We still prefer to describe what the system builds and proves, rather than relying on a label.

L-TOPIC is a Project-Centric Intelligence (PCI), built on a Cognitive Architecture for Intelligence (CAI), designed as a Controlled Cognitive Intelligence (CCI): coherent, traceable, verifiable, and controllable.
Physical AI ready. L-TOPIC is designed as a hardware-independent cognitive core: it can inhabit multiple bodies (humanoid robots, mobile machines, industrial arms, exoskeletons), adapting to available sensors while keeping project memory, decision continuity, and control.

The body becomes interchangeable. The intelligence persists.

Human-like intelligence implies limits — and that is the point

If “AGI” means intelligence comparable to a human, several observations follow: humans do not learn by massive constant recomputation; they do not endlessly re-learn what is already known; they progress through capitalization, procedures, rules, and feedback loops. They rely on tools rather than complete understanding of the world.

In that frame, trying to build an AI that “understands everything” or “learns everything” is not just unrealistic — it is not representative of real human intelligence.

What we do

Build a system that reasons in context, keeps continuity, chooses actions, verifies results, and improves through experience. The objective is reliable decision-making over time.

What we do not do

We do not chase “omniscience”. We do not aim to “learn the whole world”. We avoid endless recomputation and focus on structured intelligence and measurable capability.

Energy sobriety: human-aligned by design

L-TOPIC’s energy sobriety is not a marketing slogan — it is a direct consequence of a human-like approach. We do not rely on a permanently-trained “army of neurons” that requires massive ongoing compute.

Instead, we prioritize what humans do: reuse what works, activate deep reasoning only when needed, and structure decisions rather than multiply costly recalculations.

Practical implications

The system is designed to capitalize knowledge and procedures, reduce redundant processing, and focus compute on decision points and verification. This supports scalable deployment and long-horizon operation.

How we evaluate progress

L-TOPIC is built as a platform: each capability is defined, tested, and improved with clear signals. We care about what the system can reliably do in real workflows, not isolated demos.

Continuity

Maintains context across sessions and long timelines without losing trajectory.

Persistent memory

Stores and retrieves structured knowledge: decisions, constraints, procedures, learnings.

World model

Keeps an internal representation of actors, rules, risks, hypotheses, updated over time.

Multi-goal reasoning

Decomposes goals, prioritizes, arbitrates trade-offs, and replans across long horizons.

Verification & control

Detects uncertainty, uses verification protocols, respects constraints and governance.

Traceability

Maintains an auditable record of actions, decisions, and evidence for reproducibility.

L-TOPIC does not try to learn the world.
It learns to decide in the world, like a competent human in a real domain.
