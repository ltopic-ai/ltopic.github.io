Vision and Research Directions

Observation

A large portion of current artificial intelligence systems relies on short-term, stateless interactions. Despite impressive capabilities, these systems struggle to maintain a coherent internal state, accumulate durable experience, and reason over extended time horizons.

This limitation restricts the emergence of long-term goal-oriented behavior and the ability to build a progressively structured understanding of the world.

Vision

L-TOPIC explores an alternative path: cognitive architectures capable of maintaining a persistent internal state, integrating experience over time, and reasoning about evolving, multi-level objectives.

The project follows a progressive and experimental research approach, aiming to understand how AGI-like functional properties may emerge from clearly identified conceptual building blocks, without premature claims.

Research Axes

1. Persistent Memory

Investigation of memory mechanisms that extend beyond single interactions, enabling the system to retain knowledge, states, and experiences usable over long periods.

2. Internal World Models

Exploration of internal representations that allow the system to build a structured model of its environment, past actions, and their consequences.

3. Long-Horizon Reasoning

Study of planning and reasoning mechanisms capable of integrating multiple, sometimes competing objectives across extended time horizons.

4. Cognitive Continuity and Coherence

Research into the stability of the system’s cognitive identity and its ability to maintain internal coherence as context, objectives, and knowledge evolve.

Positioning

L-TOPIC does not position itself as a completed Artificial General Intelligence. The project adopts a cautious, functional approach focused on exploring, measuring, and understanding properties associated with AGI, without excessive claims or marketing narratives.

The emphasis is placed on conceptual clarity, reproducibility, and incremental evaluation of hypotheses.
