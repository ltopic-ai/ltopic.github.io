Long-horizon, decision-oriented AI platform

L-TOPIC is designed for situations where single-shot answers are not enough: long-running projects, complex decisions, and evolving contexts. The goal is to make intelligence operate over time — not just at prompt level.

Vision & criteria → Concept & architecture Contact

Key capability — Persistent memory

Structured, reusable memory: decisions, constraints, procedures and learnings — not just logs. Continuity across sessions and projects.

Key capability — World model

An internal representation of context (actors, rules, risks, hypotheses), continuously updated to reason beyond immediate input.

Key capability — Long-horizon multi-goal reasoning

Decomposition, prioritization, arbitration and replanning across long trajectories, with continuity.

The term “AGI” has no single scientific definition. Some actors define their own criteria and claim AGI accordingly. Under functional definitions, L-TOPIC could qualify as AGI-like.

Our approach is different. L-TOPIC is a Project-Centric Intelligence (PCI), based on a Cognitive Architecture for Intelligence (CAI), with a strong emphasis on Controlled Cognitive Intelligence (CCI): traceability, coherence, and governance.

This direction naturally leads to sobriety: not by “marketing”, but because we aim to mirror how humans operate — capitalizing on experience, procedures and tools rather than massive re-learning through brute force.

Positioning

L-TOPIC does not chase a mythical, omniscient super-intelligence. It targets a human-realistic form of intelligence: situated, specialized, persistent, and capable of deciding effectively in concrete environments over time.
