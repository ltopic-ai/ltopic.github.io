Why L-TOPIC exists

Most AI systems optimize for immediate answers. Real organizations face a different problem: decisions must remain coherent over time.

Projects span weeks or months. Context evolves. Constraints change. Responsibility remains.

L-TOPIC exists to address this gap: to make artificial intelligence operate across time, not just across prompts.

Key capability — Persistent memory

A structured, reusable memory: decisions, constraints, procedures, learnings — not just logs.

Key capability — World model

An internal representation of context (actors, rules, risks, hypotheses) that updates over time.

Key capability — Long-horizon multi-goal reasoning

Decomposition, prioritization, arbitration, and replanning across long trajectories, with continuity.

Technical founders

Regulated or high-responsibility domains

Long-horizon thinkers

A platform, not a demo

L-TOPIC aims to be a reusable platform to build reliable agents: tool orchestration, observability, evaluation, operational safety, and extensibility. The goal is not to produce a flashy answer, but to drive a trajectory over time.

1) Memory

Short / mid / long-term, structured, versioned, traceable, with targeted recall.

2) World model

Context, constraints, hypotheses, risks: an internal representation updated as actions unfold.

3) Planning & decision

Goals → sub-goals → tasks: prioritization, arbitration, replanning, trajectory maintenance.

4) Orchestration & evidence

Tools, workflows, action logs, outputs, and evidence for reproducible execution.

5) Verification & control

Uncertainty detection, “I don’t know”, verification protocols, execution constraints.

6) Continuous evaluation

Scenarios, metrics, non-regression: improvement through loops and feedback.

Energy sobriety (as a consequence)

L-TOPIC favors an approach inspired by real human intelligence: capitalizing what already works, activating heavy reasoning only when necessary, and structuring decision-making rather than multiplying brute-force recomputation.

Sobriety is a consequence of the architecture — not a slogan. Details are available on the Vision and Concept pages.
