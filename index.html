Long-horizon, decision-oriented AI platform

L-TOPIC designs an artificial intelligence platform for situations where single-shot answers are not sufficient: long-running projects, complex decisions, evolving contexts. The goal is to make intelligence operate over time, not only “at prompt level”.

Pragmatic approach: each capability is defined, observed, tested and improved (memory, planning, verification).

Vision & criteria → Concept & architecture

Key capability — Persistent memory

Structured, reusable memory: decisions, constraints, procedures, learnings — not just a chat history.

Key capability — World model

An internal representation of context (actors, rules, risks, hypotheses) updated over time.

Key capability — Long-horizon multi-goal

Decomposition, prioritization, arbitration and replanning across long trajectories, with continuity.

Positioning (clear)

The term AGI currently has no single scientific definition. Many actors rely on self-defined criteria (memory, autonomy, planning, continuity). According to some commonly cited criteria (persistent memory, continuity, multi-goal reasoning), L-TOPIC could qualify as AGI.

Our deliberate choice is to precisely describe what the system builds and what it can demonstrate, rather than claiming a label.

L-TOPIC is defined as a Project-Centric Intelligence (PCI), built on a Cognitive Architecture for Intelligence (CAI), designed as a Controlled Cognitive Intelligence (CCI): coherent, traceable, verifiable and governable.

A platform, not a demo

L-TOPIC aims to be a reusable platform for building reliable agents: tool orchestration, observability, evaluation, operational safety and extensibility. The challenge is not to produce an impressive answer, but to steer a trajectory over time.

1) Memory

Short / mid / long-term memory, structured, versioned, traceable, with targeted recall.

2) World model

Context, constraints, hypotheses, risks: an internal representation updated as actions unfold.

3) Planning & decision

Goals → sub-goals → tasks: prioritization, arbitration, replanning, trajectory control.

4) Orchestration & evidence

Tools, workflows, action logs, outputs and evidence for reproducible execution.

5) Verification & control

Uncertainty detection, “I don’t know”, verification protocols, execution constraints.

6) Continuous evaluation

Scenarios, metrics, non-regression: improvement through feedback loops.

Energy sobriety (signal)

L-TOPIC favors an approach inspired by real human intelligence: capitalizing on what already works, activating reasoning only when necessary, and structuring decision-making rather than multiplying recomputation.

Energy efficiency is a consequence of the architecture, not a marketing slogan. Details are presented in the Vision and Concept pages.
